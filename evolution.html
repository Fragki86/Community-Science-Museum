Darwin's theory

Charles Darwin (1809â€“1882) was an English naturalist that developed a theory regarding the biological evolution, stating that all species of organisms arise and develop through the natural selection of small, inherited variations that increase the individual's ability to compete, survive, and reproduce. Also called Darwinian theory, it originally included the broad concepts of transmutation of species or of evolution which gained general scientific acceptance after Darwin published On the Origin of Species in 1859.

Story of our species

Follow the story of Wooly the Mammoth and learn about the extinction of different species as well as how they were living millions of years ago. Dodos are here to tell you their story and show you how many species that lived a long time ago have evoluted and adapted to climate change and the needs of the millenial change.

In this exhibition you will see unique dinosaur fossils also and you can go through our interactive platform to experience the different kinds of them. You can use as well our newly developed VR system to jump in that period of time and compare yourself with all these species.
